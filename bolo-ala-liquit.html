The italian classic! 😍

I've never been to the southern parts of Italy. And during the few times I've been in Tirol, Genova or Pisa, I didn't have the opportunity to try a genuine italian Bolognese. Since it was one of the first dishes I was able to cook though, this recipe comes with the refinement of a more than 25 years lasting quest for the best possible Bolgnese!

Don't start with this recipe if you are hungry already!
It will be on the stove for a couple of hours, so make sure your stomach isn't empty before you give it a go. Like a Lasagna, the Bolognese tends to taste even better the day after it was prepared!

Final note: It is crucial to have fresh herbs at hand. The difference between 95ct dried stuff from the discounter of your choice and a handful of fresh herbs can't be emphasized enough! 😋

Ingredients

  • 500g minced beef
  • 100g bacon
  • 2 onions
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 3 fresh tomatoes
  • 2 ts tomato paste
  • 500g soup greens - carrots, celery, leek
  • 1 btl italian red wine
  • olive oil
  • 500g italian spaghetti

spices and herbs

  • salt, pepper
  • mild red paprika, cinnamon
  • tyme, oregano, basil, tarragon

Steps

  1. Prepare bacon, onions, tomatoes and soup greens, cut in small cubes, cut garlic together with terragon extra fine
  2. Roast onions in a large pan, add bacon and minced meat and one third of garlic/terragon mix as well as the tomato paste, season with salt, pepper and mild red paprika. Pour on small portions of red wine and stir every two minutes
  3. Add tomatoes and soup greens and pour on a cup of lew water and another lavish dash of red wine.
  4. Let it simmer on low temparature for at least two hours. Add the minced herbes and the rest of garlic/terragon mixture. Season again with salt, pepper and paprika. Add a small chili if you like a subtle hot tingle on your tongue 😉
